Property Detail Page

Optimizing the Property Detail Page to reduce support tickets.

Back
2024 Property Detail Page
Intro Research Definitions Testing Results
My Role Senior Product Designer
Time 2 months
Team
1x Designer 1x Product Manager 6x Developers
INTRO

Context

Support tickets went up by 76% (122 in total) — 25% (30 tickets) were related to problems around the PDP.

Problem

We grouped the issues into 3 main problems:

  • Confusing information architecture and page structure.
  • Workflows weren't intuitive.
  • Poor performance.

Plus: UI wasn't consistent with new brand guidelines.

Goal

Reduce support team's workload related to PDP by improving stability and usability.

KR#1 - PERFORMANCE Maintain section Apdex up to ≥0.95.
KR#2 - USABILITY Complete-publish: reduce task-flow by 50% (21 min AVG).
KR#3 - OUTCOME Reduce tickets related to PDP by at least 70%.

Hypothesis

We believe that by simplifying the property detail page, support tickets will decrease, because users will experience less friction.

RESEARCH

Discovery

I did 4 different analyses to understand both the users' daily routines and the similar solutions that already exist in the market.

Interviews

I interviewed advisors from Argentina and Mexico to learn about their workflow, their contact with clients and to find their pain points.

Benchmark

I analyzed the publishing flow of 4 players to identify design patterns and understand how they handle the entire process.

Card sorting

Validated with 10 advisors which information was most useful for them when they access to a property detail page.

Survey

To support the card sorting, we ran a survey to check what features clients look for the most.

DEFINITIONS

Userflows

I defined the user flow for the main tasks, then validated it with stakeholders and moved forward with the app's design and experience.

TESTING

Testing & Validation

We tested the solution using high-fidelity design due to: tight deadline and non-complex flows.

STEP 1

Set up and hunting

We defined goals and dynamic that the user tests would have. I recruited 8 users, created 2 scripts, and built a template to take notes quickly and make the analysis easier later.

STEP 2

Testing

I ran the remote testing sessions with each user individually.

STEP 3

Results & Prioritization

The interface demonstrated strong usability, with 6 out of 8 users completing tasks smoothly and 7 out of 8 understanding the information clearly, though 17 minor adjustments were identified for refinement.

#1 Grouped them by section and prioritized them based on: recurrence in the tests & daily impact on the user.
#2 Defined potential solutions to P1 & P2.
#3 Validate solutions with the rest of the team.
#4 Iterated on the design and left it ready for implementation.
